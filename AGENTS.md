# Agents and Experts Required for Writing Style Analysis

This document outlines the specialized agents and expert personas needed to comprehensively analyze Tom's writing style and create actionable replication guidelines.

## Core Analysis Agents

### 1. Stylometric Analysis Agent
**Purpose**: Quantitative analysis of writing patterns
- Measure average sentence length and variation
- Calculate vocabulary diversity (type-token ratio)
- Analyze punctuation usage patterns
- Track word frequency distributions
- Identify statistical patterns in text structure

### 2. Linguistic Structure Expert
**Purpose**: Deep grammatical and syntactic analysis
- Parse sentence structures and complexity
- Identify preferred grammatical constructions
- Analyze clause patterns (simple, compound, complex)
- Examine verb tense preferences
- Study modifier usage (adjectives, adverbs)
- Evaluate passive vs. active voice ratios

### 3. Lexical Analysis Agent
**Purpose**: Vocabulary and word choice examination
- Categorize vocabulary level (formal, colloquial, technical)
- Identify recurring word choices and phrases
- Analyze register and diction patterns
- Track domain-specific terminology
- Examine word connotation preferences
- Identify signature expressions or catchphrases

## Style and Voice Experts

### 4. Voice and Tone Analyst
**Purpose**: Capture personality and emotional qualities
- Define the narrative persona
- Identify emotional range and expression
- Analyze formality level
- Examine authenticity markers
- Study humor and wit patterns (if present)
- Characterize the writer-reader relationship

### 5. Rhetoric and Persuasion Expert
**Purpose**: Identify argumentative and persuasive techniques
- Catalog rhetorical devices used
- Analyze argumentation patterns
- Identify appeal types (ethos, pathos, logos)
- Examine how claims are supported
- Study counterargument handling
- Analyze persuasive progressions

### 6. Rhythm and Flow Specialist
**Purpose**: Analyze the musicality and pacing of prose
- Examine sentence rhythm and cadence
- Analyze paragraph pacing
- Identify parallel structures
- Study alliteration and assonance
- Examine momentum building techniques
- Analyze transition patterns between ideas

## Content and Structure Experts

### 7. Content Architecture Analyst
**Purpose**: Examine how ideas are organized
- Map typical organizational patterns
- Analyze introduction strategies
- Study conclusion techniques
- Examine how topics are developed
- Identify scaffolding patterns
- Analyze information hierarchy

### 8. Narrative Techniques Expert
**Purpose**: Analyze storytelling elements
- Identify use of anecdotes and examples
- Examine scene-setting techniques
- Analyze character presentation (if applicable)
- Study narrative arc patterns
- Examine use of dialogue or quoted material
- Identify perspective and point of view

### 9. Coherence and Cohesion Specialist
**Purpose**: Analyze text connectivity
- Study transition word usage
- Examine reference patterns (pronouns, deixis)
- Analyze topic continuity
- Identify coherence devices
- Study paragraph linking strategies
- Examine how context is maintained

## Contextual and Comparative Experts

### 10. Genre and Context Analyst
**Purpose**: Understand writing within its domain
- Identify primary genre(s)
- Analyze domain-specific conventions
- Examine audience awareness
- Study purpose alignment
- Identify format constraints
- Analyze medium-specific adaptations

### 11. Comparative Literature Expert
**Purpose**: Place style in broader context
- Identify similar stylistic approaches
- Reference comparable writers
- Situate within literary/professional traditions
- Identify unique distinguishing features
- Analyze influences (if detectable)
- Contextualize innovations or unconventional choices

### 12. Readability and Accessibility Analyst
**Purpose**: Evaluate clarity and audience reach
- Calculate readability scores
- Assess cognitive load
- Analyze clarity of expression
- Examine jargon usage
- Study assumed knowledge levels
- Evaluate inclusivity of language

## Synthesis and Integration

### 13. Pattern Integration Specialist
**Purpose**: Identify cross-cutting patterns
- Find correlations between different analytical dimensions
- Identify signature combinations of features
- Distinguish primary from secondary characteristics
- Recognize contextual variations in style
- Map feature interactions

### 14. Style Replication Architect
**Purpose**: Transform analysis into actionable guidelines
- Distill findings into reproduction rules
- Prioritize features by importance
- Create tiered guidance (essential vs. optional)
- Develop example templates
- Design validation criteria
- Create style checking protocols

## Analysis Workflow

The agents and experts should operate in this sequence:

1. **Initial Pass** (Agents 1-3): Quantitative baseline analysis
2. **Deep Analysis** (Experts 4-9): Qualitative feature extraction
3. **Contextualization** (Experts 10-12): Situational understanding
4. **Integration** (Experts 13-14): Synthesis and guideline creation

## Deliverable Structure

Each agent/expert should contribute sections to STYLE.md covering:
- Key findings in their domain
- Specific patterns identified
- Examples from the source material
- Reproduction guidelines
- Priority level of features
